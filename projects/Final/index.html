Buffalo Bills Draft Picks

Introduction

The Buffalo Bills entered the 2024 draft, looking to build depth in their team and fill a handful of positions. The General Manager, Brandon Beane selected 10 new players, their statistics can be seen below.

'Right here, right now'

By: Jordan Fine

Round 2, Pick No. 33 - Keon Coleman, wide receiver, Florida State

Arguably a first round pick, Keon attended Michigan State University before transferring to Florida State and playing wide receiver. At 6 '4 and 210 pounds, the wide receiver made 11 touchdowns in his 2023 season. He ran 658 yards receiving and made 50 receptions. At only 20 years old, Coleman serves as a new force and fills a much needed spot for the Bills after the departure of their old wide receiver Stefon Diggs. While his speed at the combine was not as fast as the other first round picks, Coleman also demonstrates a stronger athletic ability by being a three sport player, also having adamant basketball skills. As for some other stats from his college career, Coleman has punted 300 yards and made 58 long receptions in his 2023 season.

Round 2, Pick No. 60 - Cole Bishop, safety, Utah

Coming in at 6 '2 and a weight of 206, Cole Bishop was selected in the second round as a safety for the Bills. His combine results posted a 4.45 40-yard dash, 39” vertical jump, and a 10’4” broad jump. At Utah in his 2023 season, Bishop assisted in 25 tackles and defended three passes. He made 60 tackles that season. Also during the 2023 season at Utah, Bishop managed nine long interceptions and three defended passes. Earning a 6.20 combine score, he is a great candidate for a backup with the potential to develop into a steller starter. Combine score: 6.20

Round 3, Pick 95 - DeWayne Carter, defensive tackle, Duke

At the combine, DeWayne Carter weighed in at 302 pounds with a height of 6’2. Carter completed the 40-yard-dash in 4.99 seconds and the 10-yard-split in 1.72 seconds. Carter is coming from Duke University where he played the 2023 season where he made 41 total tackles and four defended passes. He was the captain of his team for three years (the longest there has ever been a single team captain) and he is now joining the Bills as a defensive tackle, in his entire career at Duke, he posted 125 tackles.

Round 4, Pick 128 - Ray Davis, running back, Kentucky

Ray Davis played college football at Kentucky and comes in at 5 '10 and 216 pounds. He had 199 rushing attempts in his 2023 season and boasted 1,129 rushing yards in a single season. He also scored 14 rushing touchdowns in his final season at Kentucky and was drafted to the Buffalo Bills as a running back. Upon analysis, he is a strong candidate for a potential starter in his upcoming years as a Bill. In his 2023 season, Davis made 12 runs of 20 or more yards which secured fans' feelings about his addition to the team. Overall, Davis played in 33 career games and ran 4,815 yards.

Round 5, Pick 141 - Sedrick Van Pran-Granger, center/guard, Georgia

Coming in at 6’4 and weighing 298 pounds, Sedrick Van Pran-Granger was selected in the fifth round for Buffalo and earned a prospect grade of 6.22, making him a potential average starter. He joined the team from Georgia and is playing center for the Bills, completing their offensive line. In his 2023 season at Georgia, Van Pran-Granger was coached in one of the toughest mindsets, making him compatible with the pros. He is a tough candidate and said on his combine prospect sheet, he is gritty and ready to work. These stats and tools should help him succeed in the o-line.

Round 5, Pick 168 - Javon Solomon, defensive end, Troy

Looking at Javon Solomon's draft and combine profile, he came in at 6 '1 and 246 pounds. He is on the smaller side for an edge player, but has the potential to develop into a starter. In his 2023 season he played ball at Troy college which is in Alabama. There he contributed to the win of three sun-belt conferences. He has a combination of traits that make him right for the job and with an experience in winning, his mindset is already there. His main trait is his pass rushing with a stat of 33 sacks at the end of his college career.

Round 6, Pick 204 - Tylan Grable, tackle, UCF

The Bills picked Tylan Grable in their sixth round and look to expand their offensive tackle depth. Grable is a 6’6 306 pound player who attended the University of Central Florida and in his 2023 season, made many appearances. In his college career at UCF and Jacksonville State, Grable started in 43 games and according to his combine results, he may serve on the Bills as guard/tackle flexibility.

Round 6, Pick 219 - Daequan Hardy, cornerback, Penn State

Daequan Hardy played football at Penn State and was picked by the Bills to add to their center-back team. In his college career, he scored three touchdowns and held 248 yards in punt returns. He finished his Penn State career tied for fourth in school history in punt return touchdowns (2) and seventh in punt return average (14.6).

Round 7, Pick 221 - Travis Clayton, offensive tackle, England - International Player Pathway

Travis Clayton has simply never played football. The Bills reasoning for drafting him was due to his elite athleticism playing rugby in Basingstoke England, where he entered the draft through the international portal and is now a Buffalo Bill.

Conclusion

Overall the Bills 2024 draft was given a grade of “B+” as their newly drafted players take to Buffalo and prepare for the upcoming season, can they live up to their predecessors?
